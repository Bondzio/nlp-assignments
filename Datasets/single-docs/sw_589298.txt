Doug Peterson

Douglas Blair "Doug" Peterson (1945 â€“ June 26, 2017) was an American yacht designer. He began with the One Tonner "Ganbare" in 1973. Peterson's designs have pioneered many innovations in racing and cruising yachts. He was born in Los Angeles, California.

Peterson later entered the America's Cup circle as a key design member of the winning 1992 "America" and 1995 "NZL 32" "Black Magic" Team New Zealand design teams. In 2000, Peterson designed the winning Louis Vuitton Cup boat for Prada Challenge.

Peterson died on June 26, 2017 in San Diego, California after a long battle with laryngeal cancer, aged 71.
