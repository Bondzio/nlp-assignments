Alma Cogan (19 May 1932 â€“ 26 October 1966) was an English singer of traditional pop in the 1950s and early 1960s. She was called "the girl with the giggle in her voice". One of her hit songs was "Sugartime". It was released in 1958 alongside The McGuire Sisters' version.

Cogan was born in the East End of London, England. She died after ovarian cancer at age thirty-four.
