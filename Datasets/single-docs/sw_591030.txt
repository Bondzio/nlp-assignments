Richard Gilbert Scott

Richard Gilbert Scott (12 December 1923 â€“ 1 July 2017) was a British architect. He was the son of Giles Gilbert Scott and great-grandson of the great Gothic Revival architect George Gilbert Scott. He was known for designing the Forth Road Bridge. He was born in Buckinghamshire, England.

Scott was educated at Harrow, Charterhouse School, Bartlett School of Architecture London University, and Regent Street Polytechnic School of Architecture. 

Scott died in Birmingham, England on 1 July 2017 at the age of 93.
