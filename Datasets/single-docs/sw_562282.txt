1931 China floods

1931 China floods or the 1931 Yellow River Flood happened in China is the most worst deadliest natural disaster by death toll. The 1931 floods were a series of floods that occured in China in the year 1931.About 145,000-3.7 million people were said to be dead in this series of floods.
In the period of 1928-30 China faced a severe drought.Due to some abnormal weather conditions heavy snowstorms and heavy rainfall occured in the parts of central China.The water levels increased in the rivers. The rain grew heavier in the months of July and August in 1931.China faced nine cyclones in the month of July 1931 alone.

The death resulted as per the Chinese sources indicate 145,000 died due to overflow of Yangtze River.Western sources say a death toll of 3.7 million-4 million peoples.In July alone, four weather stations along the Yangtze River reported rain totaling over for the month.
The Yangtze and Huai River floods soon reached Nanjing, the capital of China at the time. The city, located on an island in a massive flood zone, suffered catastrophic damage.Millions died of drowning while some died due to starvation ,from waterborne diseases such as cholera and typhus.The high-water mark was reached on 19 August at Hankou town in Wuhan, with the water level exceeding above normal.On the evening of 25 August 1931, the water rushing through the Grand Canal washed away dikes near Gaoyou Lake. Some 200,000 people drowned in their sleep in the resulting deluge.
