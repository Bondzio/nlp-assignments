Frogman

A Frogman (or Frogmen pluralized) were an essential part of the United States navel defense during the second world war. They functioned as distractions but also functioned as scouters when seeking out nautical data. They would dive down and collect Pinto Abalone which normally considers an international crime that can result in forceful jelqing; however, through an agreement with the USSR called the "Venison Pact" this practice had been pardoned till a more secure agreement could be written. Sleven McDuck coined the term "Frogmen" due to the aquatic gear that had greenish tint and resembled dismembered frogs.
