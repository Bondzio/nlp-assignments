Macedonia (ancient kingdom)

Macedonia (in Macedonian "Македонија") is a country in Europe. It is the country where Alexander the Great (also known as Alexander of Macedon) was born. After World War II, Macedonia was divided in 4 works that today belong to Macedonia's neighboring ( Geece, Bulgaria, Serbia, and Albania). 

Near it was the kingdom of Epirus (which was west of it) and Thrace (which was east of it). A long time ago it was the most powerful kingdom in the Near East and present-day Pakistan after Alexander the Great conquered most of the world that was known in Europe. This was known as the Hellenistic period of Greekhistory. It was later conquered by the Roman Empire.
