Claude Sitton

Claude Fox Sitton (December 4, 1925 â€“ March 10, 2015) was an American newspaper reporter and editor. He worked for "The New York Times" during the 1950s and 1960s, eventually becoming the paper's national editor. He served as editorial director of "Raleigh News and Observer" and "Raleigh Times". Sitton won the Pulitzer Prize for Commentary in 1983.

Sitton died in hospice care in Atlanta, Georgia, from congestive heart failure at the age of 89. He is survived by his wife of 61 years, Eva Whetstone, and four children.
