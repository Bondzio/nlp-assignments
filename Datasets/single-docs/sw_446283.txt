Andrew Wyeth

Andrew Newell Wyeth ( ; July 12, 1917 â€“ January 16, 2009) was an American visual artist, primarily a realist painter, working mostly in a regionalist style. He was one of the best-known U.S. artists of the middle 20th century.

Wyeth was born in Chadds Ford, Pennsylvania. He was the youngest of N.C. Wyeth's five children. His siblings included artists Henriette Wyeth and Carolyn Wyeth as well as inventor Nathaniel Wyeth.

Wyeth painted illustrations for the classics, Treasure Island, Robinson Crusoe, and The Last of the Mohicans.

On January 16, 2009, Wyeth died in his sleep in Chadds Ford after a brief illness. He was 91 years old.
