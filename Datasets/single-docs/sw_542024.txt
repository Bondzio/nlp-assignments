Bourgogne-Franche-Comté

Bourgogne-Franche-Comté (meaning "Burgundy–Free County") is one of the administrative regions of France. It is in eastern France and was formed in 2014 from two old regions: Bourgogne and Franche-Comté. "Aquitaine-Limousin-Poitou-Charentes" was the temporary name for the region.

Its capital (préfecture) is Dijon but the council of the region meets in Besançon.

The Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region is the sixth largest region of France with an area of . It is in northeastern France and borders to the north with the Grand Est region, to the west with Île-de-France and Centre-Val de Loire regions, to the south with the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region, and to the east with Switzerland (the cantons of Vaud, Neuchâtel and Jura).
