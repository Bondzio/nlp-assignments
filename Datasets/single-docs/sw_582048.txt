Night of terror

The Night of Terror refers to November 14, 1917 when several women were arrested for protesting in front of the White House for Women's Rights. This event is most known for the brutal torture of women in the Occoquan Workhouse in Virginia. The 300+ activists involved were fighting against President Woodrow Wilson for the right to vote. 33 women were thrown in jail and these women were the ones who experienced the night of terror. Lucy Burns, one of the founders of the NWP was chained to her cell with her arms in the air. The other co founder Alice Paul was held down on a chair and had a long tube shoved down her throat, they forced fed her mushed up food until she threw up. Many of the other women died of lack of air, the beatings and of heart attacks and strokes. They got their rights on August 26, 1920.
