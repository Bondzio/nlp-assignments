Olympe de Gouges

Olympe de Gouges (1748-1793), born Marie Gouze, was a French playwright and feminist activist. In 1791, she wrote "" ("Declaration of the rights of Woman and of the Female Citizen") in response to the "Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen". 

In 1784 she wrote a play that supported slavery being abolished (gotten rid of). It was performed as ' ("Slavery of the negroes") in December 1789. It shut down after three performances. It was published in 1792 as ' ("Slavery of the blacks").
