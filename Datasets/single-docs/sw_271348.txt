Francesco Maria de' Medici, Duke of Rovere and Montefeltro

Medici was born in Florence. He was the second son of Ferdinando II de' Medici, Grand Duke of Tuscany.

In 1683 he was made governor of Siena. 

In 1686, Pope Innocent XII made him a cardinal. In 1709, he was permitted to give up his role as cardinal on July 14 1709 the same year.

In March 1694, he inherited to the Duchies of Rovere and Montefeltro. 

In 1709, he married Eleonora Luisa Gonzaga.

Medici died at the Bagno a Ripoli in 1711.
