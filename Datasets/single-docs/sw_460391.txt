Albert Reynolds

Albert Reynolds (3 November 1932 – 21 August 2014) was a former Irish politician. He was twice Taoiseach of Ireland. He served from February 1992 to January 1993 and again from January 1993 to December 1994. He was the fifth leader of Fianna Fáil during the same period. He has been nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize.

Reynolds died in Dublin, Ireland from complications from Alzheimer's disease, aged 81.
