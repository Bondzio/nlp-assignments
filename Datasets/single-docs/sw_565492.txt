ER (TV series)

ER was an American medical drama series on NBC. The show aired from September 19, 1994 to April 2, 2009.

The show was about fictional County General Hospital in Chicago. It showed stories from the emergency room.

Novelist Michael Crichton was the creator of the show.

"ER" won 23 Primetime Emmy Awards. It won 116 awards overall, including the Peabody Award. It was nominated for 124 Emmys.
