Río Limpio, Elías Piña

Río Limpio (in English, "Clean River") is a Dominican municipal district of Pedro Santana in the Elías Piña Province.

The municipality had, in 2010, a total population of 3,364: 1,913 men and 1,451 women. The urban population was of the total population.

Río Limpio became a municipal district of Pedro Santana by the law 36 of 15 November 1992.

Río Limpio has an area of . It is on the right side of the "Vallecito" river, a left tributary of the Artibonite river.

The town is to the west of the "Loma Nalga de Maco" mountain, high (), the highest mountain in the western Cordillera Central mountain range.

The limits of the municipality are the Dajabón province to the northwest, the Santiago Rodríguez province to the northeast, the San Juan province to the east, the municipality of Pedro Santana to the south and Haiti to the west.

The main economic activity of the municipality is farming. The main crops are coffee and rice.
