Patriarch Bartholomew I of Constantinople

Ecumenical Patriarch Bartholomew I (born 29 February 1940) is the Ecumenical Patriarch of Constantinople, since 2 November 1991. 

He is the "first among equals" in the Eastern Orthodox Communion, and the spiritual leader of 300 million Orthodox Christians around the world.

Bartholomew wrote a chapter with Pope John Paul II in “Earth Toward Our Children’s Future". 

He wrote an essay, "To Commit a Crime Against the Natural World Is a Sin” in "Moral Ground: Ethical Action for a Planet in Peril" (Trinity University Press, 2010 ISBN 9781595340665).
