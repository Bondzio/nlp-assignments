Lom, Norway

Lom is a municipality in Oppland county, Norway. It is part of the traditional region of Gudbrandsdal. The administrative center of the municipality is the village of Fossbergom. The municipality of Lom was established on 1 January 1838. The area of Skjåk was separated from Lom to become a municipality of its own in 1866.

Lom is famous for its extensive history. It has one of the few remaining stave churches in Norway. The church was built in 1158. Lom sits at the base of two mountains. "Glittertind" is and "Galdhøpiggen" is high.
