Emily Browning

Emily Jane Browning (born 7 December 1988) is an Australian film actress and singer. Browning made her film start in the Australian television film "The Echo of Thunder," later, she played recurring roles in Australian television shows "Blue Heelers" and "Something in the Air." Her breakthrough role was in the 2002 horror film "Ghost Ship," which introduced her to a wider audience. In 2005, Browning won the Australian Film Institute International Award for Best Actress for her portrayal of Violet Baudelaire in the comedy film "Lemony Snicket's A Series of Unfortunate Events" (2004). 

Browning is also known for her roles in the horror film "The Uninvited" (2009), the action film "Sucker Punch" (2011), and the independent film "Sleeping Beauty" (2011). Her role in "Sleeping Beauty" was highly praised and in 2011 was named the Breakthrough performer of the year by Hamptons International Film Festival.
