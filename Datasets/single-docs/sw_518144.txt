Warm Leatherette (Grace Jones album)

Warm Leatherette is the fourth studio album by Jamaican recording artist Grace Jones. The album was released on 9 May 1980 through Island Records.

For the album Jones worked with reggae producers Sly and Robbie. The album is different from Jones' first three albums as they were mainly disco and R&B. This album had reggae and electronica sounds. Chris Blackwell planned to make a record with "a harsh sound that was heavy with Jamaican rhythm".
