Amit

Amit is a common male given name. It is common in India, Nepal, Israel, Pakistan, Iran, Middle East, and Bangladesh.

In Hindi and Bengali, Amit () means infinite or immeasurable or boundless. It is part of Amitabha Buddha. It is one of the 108 names of the Hindu God Shri Ganesha. It also means an eternal friend of everyone in Hindi, Nepali and Sanskrit.

In Hebrew, Amit () means colleague, friend.

In Arabic, Amit () means being highly praised, beloved and victorious. In Islam Amit refers to the traditional Arabian gesture. During battles the Muslim army yelled "Yā manṣūr Amit!" and attacked.
