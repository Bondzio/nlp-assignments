Halim El-Dabh

Halim Abdul Messieh El-Dabh ( ("Ḥalīm ʻAbd al-Masīḥ al-Ḍab"ʻ); born March 4, 1921) is an Egyptian-born American composer, performer, ethnomusicologist, and educator. He has a career spanning six decades. He is known as an early pioneer of electronic music, for having composed in 1944 the first piece of electronic tape music.
