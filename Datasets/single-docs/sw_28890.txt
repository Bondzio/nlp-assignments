Abkhazia

Abkhazia (/Apkhazeti) is a partially recognized republic in the Caucasus. As of 2009, about 215,000 people lived there. The capital is Sukhumi. Most countries consider Abkhazia to be part of Georgia. It is recognized only by Russia, Nauru, Nicaragua and Venezuela.

Abkhazia covers an area of about at the northwest part of Greater Caucasus. The Caucasus Mountains to the north and the northeast divide Abkhazia from the Russian Federation.
