Sujata Trivedi

Sujata Trivedi () is an Indian singer whose music is played in movie soundtracks. She has made music for over 25 Bollywood movies. She began singing when she was seven years old. Trivdei's training in classical music began under Prabhakar Dhakde in Nagpur, Padmashree Ustaad Ghulaam Mustafa Khan, Pt. Ajay Pohankar, and Ustad Asad Ali Khan. She has learned various aspects of light music under Pt. Satyanarayan Mishra, Gautam Mukherji and Shri Kalyanjibhai. She recorded with A. R. Rahman songs like the title track of "Dil Ne Jise Apna Kaha", "Boondon Se Baatein", and classical-based "Yeh Jo Zindagi Hai" from "1947: Earth". She also recorded "In Saahab Ko Zara Dekho" for "Calcutta Mail" with Viju Shah.
Trivdei has sung in several languages, including Marathi, Bhojpuri, Manipuri, Bengali, Gujarati, Punjabi, Tamil, Malayalam, Nepali, Rajasthani and Oriya.

She has performed more than 1600 stage shows all over India and overseas. She performed individually and in association with other playback singers, such as Nitin Mukesh, Sonu Nigam, Manhar Udhas, Kumar Sanu, Vinod Rathod, Babul Supriyo, Kavita Krishnamurthi, Ravindra Jain, Abhijit, Shaan and Sunidhi.
