Vinča culture

The Vinča culture was a culture during the stone age, rougly from 5400 BC to 4600 BC. It is named for a large site found close to modern-day Belgrade, Serbia. In addition to the territory of modern-day Serbia, sites have also been discovered in Romania, Hungary and Bosnia. Today, it is known for many ceramic figurines. Some of these figurines also have symbols on them, which have been interpreted as an early form of writing. These symbols are called Vinča symbols. 

The Vinča culture was the first to develop the knowledge and skills to process copper, in the late 6th mililenium BC.
