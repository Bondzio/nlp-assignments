Rosalyn Yalow

Rosalyn Yalow was an American medical physicist. 

Yalow was born on July 19, 1921 in New York City. Yalow graduated from Hunter College in 1941. She also went to a all-female college in New York called Hunter College. Yalow was Jewish. This made it difficult to become a scientist, due to prejudice. She continued trying. Yalow got a PHD from the University of Illinois. 

She became a consultant in nuclear physics at the Bronx Veteran’s Administration Hospital in 1947. Yalow served in the position of assistant chief of radioisotope service in 1950. She was made a research professor at the Sinai School of Medicine in New York City in 1968. In 1973, she was made head of Solomon A. Berson Research Laboratory at the Bronx Veteran’s Administration Hospital. 

She won the Nobel Prize for physiology or medicine in 1977. Rosalyn Yalow was the second woman ever to win the Nobel Prize for physiology or medicine. She shared the award with Roger Guillemin and Andrew Schally. She was famous for the creation of the radioimmunoassay (RIA). The RIA labeled things in the blood with radioactive marks. Yalow and Solomon Berson first used the RIA in 1959. It was first used to study the insulin in blood of diabetics. This was a big advancement that helped many people with diabetes. Yalow died on May 30, 2011.
