Time in North Korea

Time in North Korea is given Pyongyang Time (PYT) which is 8.5 hours ahead of Coordinated Universal Time (UTC). If it is 4 p.m. UTC, it is 12:30 a.m. the next day in North Korea. It has never observed summer time.

North Korea was originally 9 hours ahead of UTC () from 1912 until 2015, just like it's neighbours South Korea and Japan. In August 2015, the government announced it will be switching to UTC+8.5 affective at midnight on 15 August.
