Halton Arp

Halton Christian "Chip" Arp (March 21, 1927 â€“ December 28, 2013) was an American astronomer. He was known for his "Atlas of Peculiar Galaxies", published in 1966. Arp was also known as a critic of the Big Bang theory and for helping a non-standard cosmology to create an intrinsic redshift.

Arp was born on March 21, 1927 in New York City. He studied at the California Institute of Technology. Arp was married three times. He had four daughters. 

Arp died on December 28, 2013 in Munich. He died from a stroke, aged 86.
