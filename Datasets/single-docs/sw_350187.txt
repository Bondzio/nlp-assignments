Paul Krugman

Paul Robin Krugman (born February 28, 1953) is an American economist and 2008 winner of the Nobel Prize in Economics.

Krugman was born to a Jewish family in Albany, New York.

He was awarded B.A. in economics from Yale University in 1974.
He earned a Ph.D. from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in 1977. 

From 1982 to 1983, he spent a year working at the Reagan White House as a staff member of the Council of Economic Advisers. 

Krugman taught at Yale, MIT, University of California, and Stanford University. He is still teaching at the City University of New York, the London School of Economics, and Princeton University. He is also a columnist for the "The New York Times".

The Nobel Prize in Economics was awarded to Krugman in 2008.
