Paul Cox

Paulus Henrique Benedictus "Paul" Cox (16 April 1940 â€“ 18 June 2016) was an award-winning Dutch-born Australian filmmaker. He was known as 'Australia's most prolific film auteur'. 

He published "Reflections: An Autobiographical Journey" in 1998.

His movie-essay "The Remarkable Mr. Kaye" (2005) is a portrait of his sick friend, the actor Norman Kaye, who appeared in many of Cox movies, such as "Lonely Hearts" and "Man of Flowers". In 2006 he became the Patron of the Byron Bay Film Festival.
