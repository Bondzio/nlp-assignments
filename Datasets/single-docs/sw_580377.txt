Kirill Kovaldzhi

Kirill Vladimirovich Kovaldzhi (; March 14, 1929 â€“ April 10, 2017) was a Russian poet, novelist, literary critic and translator.

In Moscow, published collections of poems "At Dawn" (1958), "Voices" (1972), "After Noon" (1981), "Yearly Rings" (1982), "High Dialogue" (1988), "Links and Grains" (1989), "The Book of Lyrics" (1993), "The Invisible Threshold" (1999), "To You. On Demand" (2002), the book of prose and poetry "Countdown" (2003), the short book "Zerna" (2005).

The author of a number of short stories, the story "Five Points on the Map" (1965), the novel "Liman Stories" (1970) - his new supplemented edition, published under the title "Candle in draft" (1996), was nominated for the State Prize of Russia.

Kovaldzhi died in Moscow on April 10, 2017, aged 88.
