Rance Howard

Rance Howard (born Harold Rance Beckenholdt; November 17, 1928) is an American actor. He has starred in movies and on television. He is the father of actor and filmmaker Ron Howard and actor Clint Howard, and grandfather of the actress Bryce Dallas Howard.

Howard was born in Duncan, Oklahoma. He studied at the University of Oklahoma. He changed his name to "Rance Howard" when he became an actor. 

His career began in 1956. Howard has appeared in many famous movies such as "Cool Hand Luke" (1967), "Chinatown" (1974), "Ed Wood" (1994), "Independence Day" (1996), "A Beautiful Mind" (2001), "Cinderella Man" (2005), "Frost/Nixon" (2008), and in "The Lone Ranger" (2013).

Howard was married to Jean Speegle 1949 until her death in 2000. In 2001, he married Judy Howard.
