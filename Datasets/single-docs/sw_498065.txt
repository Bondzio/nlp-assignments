Lewis Howard Latimer

Lewis Howard Latimer (September 4, 1848 â€“ December 11, 1928) was an African-American inventor and draftsman.

Latimer's family were slaves when a local minister led them to freedom. When he was 16 Latimer lied about his age to join the U.S Navy. 

Latimer started out working at Crosby and Gould, a patent law firm. While at the law firm, Latimer invented an improved railroad car bathroom and an early air conditioning unit. Later, Latimer helped Alexander Graham Bell with the development of his patent for the first telephone. Latimer also helped Thomas Edison with his patent for the light bulb.

Latimer died on December 11, 1928 in Flushing Queens, New York at the age 80.
