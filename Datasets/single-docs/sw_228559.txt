Eazy-E

Eazy-E (real name: Eric Lynn Wright) (September 7, 1964 â€“ March 26, 1995) was an American rapper and actor. He rose to fame with the Rap group N.W.A. and is one of the founders of the "Gangsta rap" subgenre. He usually rapped about guns, drugs, relations between residents and the police, and sexual activity. 

On March 26, 1995 Eazy-E died of AIDS-related pneumonia.

He was buried at Rose Hills Memorial Park in Whittier, California.
