LGBT rights in the United Kingdom

Lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender (LGBT) rights in the United Kingdom have improved a lot in recent years. In 2005 civil partnerships for same sex couples was legalised across the UK. In 2014 same sex marriages was legalised in England, Scotland and Wales . Gay people cannot get married in Northern Ireland because the Northern Ireland assembly voted against legalising it .

Homosexuality was illegal in the UK. In 1967 it was legalised in England and Wales. In 1981 it was legalised in Scotland and in 1982 it was legalised in Northern Ireland.
