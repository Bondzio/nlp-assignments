Rainer Werner Fassbinder

Rainer Werner Fassbinder (; 31 May 1945 – 10 June 1982) was a German movie director, screenwriter, and actor. He is one of the most important figures in the New German Cinema. He was born in Bad Wörishofen, Bavaria, Germany.

Fassbinder died at the age of 37 from a heart attack caused by a lethal cocktail of cocaine and barbiturates in Munich, Germany.
