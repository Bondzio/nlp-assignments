Ned O'Gorman

Edward Charles "Ned" O'Gorman (September 26, 1929 â€“ March 7, 2014) was an American poet and educator. Some of his best known works are "The Harvesters' Vase" (1968), "The Flag the Hawk Flies" (1972), "The Wilderness and the Laurel Tree: A Guide for Teachers and Parents on the Observation of Children" (1972) and "Prophetic Voices: Ideas and Words on Revolution" (1969). He was born in New York City, New York and was raised in both Southport, Connecticut and Bradford, Vermont.

O'Gorman died from pancreatic cancer on March 7, 2014 at his home in Manhattan. He was 84 years old.
