John Doar

John Michael Doar (December 3, 1921 â€“ November 11, 2014) was an American lawyer and senior counsel with the law firm Doar Rieck Kaley & Mack in New York. He was known as being the Assistant Attorney General for Civil Rights from 1960 to 1967, during the civil rights years of the administrations of presidents John F. Kennedy and Lyndon B. Johnson. 

Doar died in New York City, New York from congestive heart failure, aged 92.
