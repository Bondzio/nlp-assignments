Marvin Hamlisch

Marvin Hamlisch (June 2, 1944 - August 6, 2012) was an American composer. He was born in Manhattan, New York City. He arranged the scores of lots of movies, including "The Sting" and "Sophie's Choice". He won an Academy Award, an Emmy Award and a Golden Globe Award, Grammy Awards, a Tony Award and a Pulitzer Prize. He died from a brief illness in Los Angeles.



In 1974, Hamlisch became the second person to win three Academy Awards in the same evening, following Billy Wilder in 1961.
