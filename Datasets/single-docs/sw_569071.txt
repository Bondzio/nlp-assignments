Place of articulation

The Place of articulation tells where a sound is made (articulated). There are at least 13 different places of articulation. They are split into four big types. 

Labial sounds are made with one or two lips.
They are split into three types:
Bilabial sounds are made with both lips.
The bilabial sounds in English are /m p b w/.
Labiodental sounds are made with the lips and the teeth.
Almost always the top lip and the bottom teeth are used.
The labiodental sounds in English are /f v/.
Linguolabial sound are made by the tongue touching the top lip.
There are no linguolabial in English.
Sometimes it is said to be part of the next group (Coronal).
