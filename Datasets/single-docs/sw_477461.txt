Where the Red Fern Grows

Where the Red Fern Grows is a 1961 novel by Wilson Rawls. It is about the friendship between a boy and two Redbone Coonhound hunting dogs.

Billy Coleman saves a Redbone Coonhound from a dog fight. He helps it recover from its wounds. He sets it free. He remembers his past. Our story starts when he remembers how he had owned two Redbone Coonhounds when he was a boy.

Dating back to his past, Billy had always wanted the dogs since he was 10. His parents however tell him they don't have enough money for them. He decides to earn the money himself. For two years, he works many different jobs. He saves $50. His grandfather helps him order the pair of Redbone Coonhounds. 

The mail does not deliver packages, so Billy goes to get them. He walks to Tahlequah, Oklahoma. He buys gifts for his family there. On the way back home, he sleeps in a cave on Sparrow Hawk Mountain. He hears a sound like a woman screaming. He realizes it is from a mountain lion. The puppies answer the roar with their howls. Billy builds a fire for safety. The next morning, he starts going back home. On the way, he sees a sycamore tree carved with the names Dan and Ann in a heart. He decides to name the puppies Old Dan and Little Ann.
