Paysandú Department

The Paysandú department () is a department of Uruguay. The capital is the city of Paysandú.

Its code is UY-PA.

The Paysandú department is in the western part of Uruguay, along the Uruguay river. It has an area of , the third largest department in the country, after the Tacuarembó and Salto departments. The population is 113,124 inhabitants (2011 census) for a population density of inhabitants/km.

There are two chains of low hills in the department. These chains of low and rounded hills are named "cuchillas" in the country. The two "cuchillas" in the department are "cuchilla de Queguay" to the northwest, and "cuchilla de Haedo" to the southeast.
