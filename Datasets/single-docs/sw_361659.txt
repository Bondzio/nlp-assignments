Jack LaLanne

Francois Henri "Jack" LaLanne (September 26, 1914 â€“ January 23, 2011) was an American inventor, writer, actor, fitness and exercise instructor and expert who is known for helping people to get fit and healthy. 

He has hosted his own show "The Jack LaLanne Show" and has appeared in cameo appearances in some movies. LaLanne was also known for swimming in the San Francisco bay with a small fishing boat tied to a rope in his mouth. 

LaLanne has a star in the Hollywood Walk of Fame. He has also received many nicknames such as "the godfather of fitness" or "first fitness superhero". LaLanne has beat Arnold Schwarzenegger in a fitness contest in his 50s. 

He was born in San Francisco, California and raised in Barkersfield, California. He was married to Irma Navarre from 1942 until they divorced in 1948, they had one daughter. Then he married Elaine Doyle fro 1959 until his death in 2011, they had two sons and one daughter. 

LaLanne died on January 23, 2011 in his Morro Bay, California home from respiratory failure caused by his pneumonia, he was 96 years old.
