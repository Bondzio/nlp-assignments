Béard-Géovreissiat

Béard-Géovreissiat is a commune in the Ain department of France. It is mostly made up of the villages of Béard and Géovreissiat. Also located in the commune is the hamlet of St Germain de Béard, and the northern part of the hamlet of Croix-Châlon.

The commune was called Géovreissiat until October 2008. Géovreissiat is the main village and is where the city hall is located.
In 2009, 935 people lived in the commune. The inhabitants are the "Béardais". 

Between 1841 and 1846, the population fell from 720 to 354 inhabitants, and continued to fall until the end of the Second World War. Today it has reached its highest population since records began.
