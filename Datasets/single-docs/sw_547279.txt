Lake Chungará

Lake Chungará (from Aymara: "chunkara" "moss on the stone") is a lake in the extreme north of Chile, in the Altiplano of the Arica y Parinacota region. It is the 29th highest lake in the world (and the 10th highest in South America).

The lake is near the volcanos Parinacota () and Pomerape (). It is part of the Lauca National Park, in the Putre comuna. The lake is at about to the east of the town of Putre and about to the west of the border with Bolivia.

In the lake and streams next to it there some small fishes that are endemic to the lake basin: a pencil catfish "Trichomycterus chungaraensis", and "Orestias chungarensis".
