Breithorn

The Breithorn is a mountain range of the Pennine Alps with its highest peak of the same name (but also called "Breithorn (Western Summit)"). It is on the border between Switzerland and Italy. It is on the main chain of the Alps. It is a halfway between the Matterhorn and Monte Rosa and east of the Theodul Pass.

The Breithorn was first climbed in 1813 by Henry Maynard (climber), Joseph-Marie Couttet, Jean Gras, Jean-Baptiste Erin and Jean-Jacques Erin.
