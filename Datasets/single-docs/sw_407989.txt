Edna St. Vincent Millay

Edna St. Vincent Millay (February 22, 1892 â€“ October 19, 1950) was an American playwright and poet. She received the Pulitzer Prize for Poetry in 1923. She was the third female to win the award. Millay was also a feminist. She used the name Nancy Boyd during her career. Millay's fame began around 1912 when she entered a poem, "Renascence", in a contest in "The Lyric Year".

Millay was born in Rockland, Maine. She died from a heart attack. She was fifty-eight.
