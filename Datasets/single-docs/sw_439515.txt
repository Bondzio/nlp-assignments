Fay McKenzie

Fay McKenzie (born February 19, 1918) is an American actress. She is best known for her leading lady roles. She appeared in two movies with Gene Autry in 1941 and 1942. 

McKenzie was the daughter of the actor Robert McKenzie and actress Eva McKenzie. One of her first roles was at the age of six in "The Dramatic Life of Abraham Lincoln" (1924) where she played Sarah Lincoln. She married screenwriter Tom Waldman.

McKenzie was born on February 19, 1918 in Hollywood, California.
