Dango

"Dango" are eaten throughout the year, but the different types are traditionally eaten in certain seasons. Three to four "dango" are often served on a skewer. One variety of "dango" from Hokkaid≈ç is made from potato flour and baked with "shoyu" (soy sauce).

A common Japanese proverb is related to the custom of cherry blossom viewing ("hanami").

There are many different varieties of "dango" which are usually named after the various seasonings served on or with it.
