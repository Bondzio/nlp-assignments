Phil Lynott

Philip Parris Lynott (20 August 1949 â€“ 4 January 1986) or Phil Lynott, was an Irish musician. He was born in West Bromwich, Staffordshire and raised in Dublin, Ireland from age four. He was a rock bass guitarist and singer, and the leader of the rock band Thin Lizzy.

Lynott started Thin Lizzy in 1969, and they continued until 1983. He also made two solo albums in the early 1980s. They were famous for their songs "Whisky in the Jar" and "The Boys Are Back in Town". He also worked with Gary Moore.

He died of septicaemia in hospital in Salisbury, Wiltshire on 4 January 1986, after suffering from drug problems.
