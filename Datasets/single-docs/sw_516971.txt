Rose Livingston

Rose Livingston (circa 1885 - after 1937) was a suffragist. She was known as the "Angel of Chinatown". She worked with prostitutes and victims of sexual slavery. 

In 1912 while trying to rescue a prostitute she was severely beaten.

In 1914 she participated in one of the Suffrage Hikes from Manhattan to Albany, New York.

In 1929, she was given a gold medal by the National Institute of Social Science.

In 1934 she was found living in poverty. A retirement fund was established for her.

In 1937 she was awarded a silver cup by Edith Claire Bryce (1880-1960) of the Peace House for her "deeds of courage without violence".
