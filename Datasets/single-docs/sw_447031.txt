Francis Gary Powers

Francis Gary Powers (August 17, 1929 – August 1, 1977) was an American pilot and spy. He was known for creating the Central Intelligence Agency U-2 spy plane which was shot down while flying a reconnaissance mission over Soviet Union airspace. He was held as Prisoner of War under the Soviet Union. This caused the 1960 U-2 incident. This played a large role in the Cold War. He was born in Jenkins, Kentucky. 

Powers died in Los Angeles County, California in a helicopter crash, aged 47.
