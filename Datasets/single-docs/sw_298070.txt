Masood Farivar

Masood Farivar (born 1969) is an Afghanistan-born writer and journalist and author of Confessions of a Mullah Warrior (Atlantic Monthly Press, 2009), which describes two teenage years while a member of the anti-Soviet Mujahideen resistance movement.

Farivar moved with his family to Pakistan in 1983. He obtained a degree from Harvard University in 1994 and subsequently worked as a business reporter for Dow Jones Newswires. According his own "Linkedin" resume, he was as of 2013, Chief of the Afghanistan Service at Voice of America and based in Washington, D.C. Previously he was with Internews Network and spent the 2011-2012 academic year as a Knight Fellow at Stanford University.
