Heimo Pfeifenberger

Heimo Pfeifenberger (born 29 December 1966 in Zederhaus, Salzburg) is a former professional footballer from Austria. He played mainly for SV Austria Salzburg and also in the Austrian national football team. With 74 goals for Salzburg and 43 for Rapid he is one of the best scorers in Austrias football history.

He started playing footballl in the youth teams of his local club USV Zederhaus. After scoring 56 goals for Zederhaus in the 1987 season he was transferred to Austria Salzburg. Later he played for SK Rapid Wien and Werder Bremen in the German Bundesliga. His most successful time was with Austria Salzburg in the mid 90s. He became two times Austrian Champion (1994 and 1995) and 1994 he was Austrias top scorer. He was also elected Footballer of the year. After playing for Bremen he returned to Austria and played again for Salzburg. 2004 he ended his active career as professional footballer. 2007 he played for SV Seekirchen in the Regionalliga West(3rd level) and as coach for SV Grödig he played 2 matches for his team.

He started as youth coach for FC Red Bull Salzburg in 2005. 2007 he became coach of SV Grödig in the Reginalliga West and promotet to the team to Erste Liga(2nd level). In the next season he was replaced and Andreas Herzog made him to assistance coach in Austrias U21 squad. After a short time with SPG Axams-Götzens he became again coach of SV Grödig in 2010.
