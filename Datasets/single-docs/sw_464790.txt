Lynsey de Paul

Lynsey de Paul (11 June 1948 â€“ 1 October 2014) was an English singer-songwriter. She was known for appearing on many Eurovision competitions. She was also known for her songs, ""Sugar Me"", ""Won't Somebody Dance With Me"", ""Storm In A Teacup"" and ""Sleeping Blue Nights"".

De Paul was born in London, England. She was never married, but was known for being in relationships with Ringo Starr, Roy Wood, James Coburn, Sean Connery, Bill Kenwright, Bernie Taupin, Chas Chandler and Dudley Moore.

De Paul suffered a brain hemorrhage on the morning of 1 October 2014. She died in a London hospital hours later, aged 66.
