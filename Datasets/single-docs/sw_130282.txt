Commodore International

Commodore is the common name for Commodore International Limited, a U.S. computer and electronics manufacturing company. Headquarters were in West Chester, Pennsylvania. In the 1960s and 70s they made adding machines and calculators. This company was important in the development of the homeâ€“personal computer in the late 1970s and 1980s. Commodore developed the first color computer sold under $300, known as the VIC-20 in 1981, and the world's best-selling desktop computer, called the Commodore 64 (1982). Commodore later released the Amiga computers in 1985. The company went bankrupt in 1994.
